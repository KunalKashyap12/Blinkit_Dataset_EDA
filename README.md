**🛒 Blinkit Orders & Delivery Analysis :**
A simple breakdown of Blinkit's order performance, delivery speeds, stock issues, and customer habits.

**📌 Project Overview :**
Quick-delivery apps depend on fast delivery and accurate store stock. This project looks at why orders get canceled, how fast deliveries actually are, when people order the most, and how revenue is generated.

The Big Takeaway: Being out of stock is the #1 reason orders get canceled, and the 10-minute delivery goal is rarely being met.

**📊 Key Insights :**
🚨 Major Problems Found
Running Out of Stock Causes Cancellations: When an item goes out of stock, cancellations jump from 9.3% to 38.7% (a 4x increase).

10-Minute Delivery Isn't Working: Only 5% of completed orders actually arrive in under 10 minutes.

**📈 Customer & Order Habits :**
Peak Hours: Most orders come in between 1:00 PM and 4:00 PM, with 2:00 PM being the busiest hour of the day.

Top Categories: Fruits & Vegetables is the most ordered category (17.8%), while Bakery items are the least ordered (8.2%).

High Customer Loyalty: Almost all customers (99.9%) order more than once, and sales are spread out evenly across users rather than coming from a few big spenders.

**❌ What Doesn't Matter :**
City & Payment Types: Cancellation rates are practically the same regardless of which city the order is in or what payment method is used.

**🎯 Top 3 Next Steps for the Business :**
Fix Inventory Tracking: Keep real-time track of store stock to prevent out-of-stock items, which will cut cancellations dramatically.

Fix the 10-Minute Delivery Promise: Either set more realistic delivery times for customers or change delivery zones to make 10 minutes actually possible.

Add Extra Staff in the Afternoon: Hire or assign more store packers and delivery drivers between 1:00 PM and 4:00 PM to handle the daily rush.
